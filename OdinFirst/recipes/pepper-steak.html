Pepper-Steak

Steak-au-poivre

The pepper-steak is a classic french dish, made with simple ingredients that when paired together develop a very coplex and teasing taste.

In the elderly times, this dish, which is better-know as "steak-au-poivre" used to be a late-night meal, and people would usually have it after a party due to the belief that the peppers had aphrodisiacs properties

Ingredients

  • Filet mignom
  • Black pepper
  • White pepper
  • Pink peppercorn
  • Rosemary
  • Butter
  • Canola oil
  • Salt
  • Heavy cream
  • Red wine

How to prepare

  1. Season the filet mignom steak with nothing but salt at all sides, including the edges
  2. Pre heat an iron cast
  3. Add a bit of canola oil
  4. Sear all edges of the steak and take it off the pan
  5. Add half of cup of red wine to deglaze the bottom of the pan
  6. Add heavy cream and let it reduce untill its very creamy
  7. Add as much pepper you want
  8. Slice the rare filet mignom in medium pieces and plate it
  9. Cover the sliced steak with the sauce, adding more grounded pepper if you want
  10. Finish with a couple leaves of rosemary
